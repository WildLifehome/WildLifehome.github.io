Smaller World

Orca

Orcas (also known as killer whales) are a type of mammal that belongs to the family of delphinidae. Orcas are considered apex predators which means that they have no natural predators. This has also earned them the nickname "wolves of the sea". While the total population of orcas around the world is 50,000, the population of orcas that live in Canada is only 74.

Peary Caribou

Peary caribou are mammanls that belong to the family of cervidae. Caribou are herbivours that feed on a variety of grasses such as Cyperaceae (sedges), lichen and mushrooms. The population of this subspecies of Caribou only reaches 13,200 mature individuals.

Black-Footed Ferret

The Black-footed ferret are mammals that belong to the Mustelidae family. Black-footed ferret are carnivores that eat other small mammals such as deer mice, sagebrush voles, meadow voles, mountain cottontails, and white-tailed jackrabbits. Black-footed ferrets are the rarest mammal in North America. In 2024, the population of Black-footed ferrets was only at 390.

Leatherback Sea Turtle

Leatherback sea turtles are marine reptiles that belong to the Dermochelyidae family. Leatherback sea turtles are omnivores, however, they mainly eat jellyfish. They are the largest living turtles in the world. The population of Leatherback sea turtles is uknown but certainly declining.

Burrowing Owl

Burrowing owls are a type of bird that belongs to the Strigidae family. Burrowing owls are carnivores, eating small animals such as insects, rodents, reptiles, amphibians, and birds. Despite this, they will also sometimes eat fruits and seeds. Sadly, there are only 270-300 breeding owls in Canada. This makes it one of the most endangeered species of bird species in Canada.

Wildlife Preservation Canada

Wildlife Preservation Canada is a wonderful non-profit organization that dedicates their work to protect endangered species in Canada. Here is some information from their website:

For nearly four decades, Wildlife Preservation Canada has been the last defence for endangered species in the country. WPC provides direct, hands-on care for reptiles, amphibians, birds, insects and mammals on the brink of extinction. WPC is the only organization in Canada to provide this critical need for multiple species in multiple recovery efforts across the country. They specialize in science-based techniques such as conservation breeding and release, reintroduction and translocation
